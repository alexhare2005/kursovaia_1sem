Our History | Trophies

Real Madrid Club de Fútbol, commonly known as Real Madrid, is a professional football club based in Madrid, Spain. Founded on March 6, 1902, by a group of football enthusiasts led by Juan Padrós, Real Madrid has grown to become one of the most successful and widely supported football clubs in the world.

Real Madrid's early years were marked by modest success in regional competitions, but the club quickly emerged as a dominant force in Spanish football during the 1950s. Under the presidency of Santiago Bernabéu, who served from 1943 to 1978, Real Madrid embarked on an ambitious project to assemble a team of world-class players, known as the 'Galácticos,' and to modernize the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium.

During this period, Real Madrid enjoyed unprecedented success, winning numerous La Liga titles and European Cups. The club's greatest era came in the late 1950s and early 1960s when they won five consecutive European Cup titles from 1956 to 1960, establishing themselves as the preeminent force in European football. Real Madrid's success has continued into the 21st century, with the club winning multiple domestic and international trophies, including La Liga titles, Copa del Rey titles, and UEFA Champions League titles. The club's rivalry with FC Barcelona, known as 'El Clásico,' is one of the most storied and intense rivalries in world football. Throughout its history, Real Madrid has been home to some of the greatest footballers of all time, including Alfredo Di Stéfano, Ferenc Puskás, Cristiano Ronaldo, and many others. The club's iconic all-white kit, inspired by a suggestion from former player Santiago Bernabéu, has become synonymous with excellence and tradition in the footballing world. Off the field, Real Madrid has also been influential, pioneering the concept of football as a global brand and establishing itself as one of the wealthiest and most valuable sports franchises in the world.

Real Madrid wrote a new chapter into the footballing history books when they got their hands on a 14th European Cup in 2022. Our team extended their dominance of the competition with a win over Liverpool in Paris. The early years of the decade also saw a 35th LaLiga title, fifth European Super Cup, eighth Club World Cup, 20th Copa del Rey and a 13th Spanish Super Cup.
